Past Projects

The Inclusion Of Young People Through Dance And Popular Songs

9-15 October 2023, San Lorenzo del Escorial

The Erasmus project had as a main activity a youth exchange where youngsters from European and Arabic countries gathered together to exchange experiences and work on the topic of inclusion using the means of dance and songs.

Respect and Equality: Acting and Communicating Together

05-13 September 2023, Tsakhkadzor, Armenia

The project aims to equip youth workers with enhanced skills and methodologies to effectively address hate speech and its detrimental effects on young individuals. By tailoring training sessions to the specific needs of diverse youth populations, including minority groups and refugees, the project endeavors to empower youth workers in facilitating proactive, group-led responses to hate-related conflicts. Additionally, it seeks to foster a deeper understanding of the impact of hate speech, encouraging youth workers to challenge oppressive power structures through youth work methods.

Youngsters Tell Digital Stories About Cultural Diversity

17-26 February, Oldemarkt, Netherlands

The project aims to foster inclusivity and intercultural dialogue by enhancing participants' understanding of discrimination, intolerance, and environmental sustainability. Through activities such as digital storytelling and photography, participants will develop skills in creativity, teamwork, and intercultural communication. Additionally, the project seeks to raise awareness of societal challenges faced by diverse cultural groups and promote active engagement in combating discrimination within communities. Finally, it aims to empower participants to explore Erasmus+ opportunities, encouraging their continued participation in future projects aimed at promoting European identity and peace.
